This book chapter addresses how individuals in a group can coordinate diverse spatial information to form a single, shared cognitive map. It discusses the theoretical underpinnings of collective spatial cognition, focusing on information integration, leadership and coordination roles, and how groups resolve conflicting spatial knowledge.
