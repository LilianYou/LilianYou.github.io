I am a Postdoctoral Research Fellow at McLean Hospital and Harvard Medical School. My research develops computational and multi-modal neuroimaging methods to understand mechanisms of brain disorders — with a primary focus on Alzheimer's disease and related dementias (ADRD), and broader applications to psychiatric conditions and aging.

My work sits at the intersection of computational neuroscience, machine learning, and clinical neuroimaging. I build data fusion frameworks that integrate neuroimaging, fluid biomarkers (e.g., cerebrospinal fluid, blood plasma), electronic health records, and other data modalities to characterize disease stages and identify targets for intervention. I am particularly interested in how multi-modal approaches can reveal mechanisms that single modalities miss — and how those mechanistic insights can be translated into clinical tools.

Before my current position, I was a research fellow at Massachusetts General Hospital and Harvard Medical School, where I worked with [Prof. Sudeshna Das](https://researchers.mgh.harvard.edu/profile/11948449/Sudeshna-Das) in the [MIND Data Science Lab](https://www.massgeneral.org/neurology/research/mind-data-science-lab) and the [Massachusetts Alzheimer's Disease Research Center](https://www.madrc.org/) (MADRC). My work there focused on applying deep learning methods — including natural language processing and neuroimaging analysis tools — to understand different stages of dementia from heterogeneous clinical data.

I received my Ph.D. in Cognitive Sciences with a concentration in Cognitive Neuroscience from the [University of California, Irvine](https://www.cogsci.uci.edu/), where I worked with [Prof. Elizabeth R. Chrastil](https://faculty.sites.uci.edu/spatialneuro/) in the [Spatial Neuroscience Lab](https://faculty.sites.uci.edu/spatialneuro/lab-members/). My doctoral research investigated the neural mechanisms of human spatial navigation — how the brain computes travel direction, trajectory planning, and spatial decision-making — using psychophysics, fMRI, and computational modeling. I also collaborated with [Prof. Jeffrey Krichmar](http://www.socsci.uci.edu/~jkrichma/) on brain-inspired computing and neurorobotics.

My research is grounded in rigorous training in experimental psychology and neuroscience — I hold a B.S. in Psychology from [South China Normal University](http://psy.scnu.edu.cn/english/) and an M.S. in Cognitive Neuroscience from [UC Irvine](https://www.cogsci.uci.edu/). I also hold an M.A. in Geography from [UC Santa Barbara](https://geog.ucsb.edu/), where I studied how people interact with and are shaped by their environments. This added dimension fundamentally informs how I approach neuroscience: I think about cognition as situated in context, shaped by environment, and varying across spatial and demographic scales. This perspective drives my interest in understanding not just how the brain works in the lab, but how brain-environment interactions contribute to real-world health outcomes.
